Capitol Reef National Park

The adventure of a lifetime awaits you!

Capitol Reef National Park is the perfect destination for those who love exploring breathtaking landscapes! From colorful canyons and towering cliffs to rock formations that seem otherworldly, this park is a must-see. The park covers nearly 241,000 acres and offers visitors a chance to see stunning geological features shaped over millions of years. It’s also less crowded than some of Utah’s other famous parks, giving you a more peaceful experience. Whether you enjoy hiking, photography, or just soaking in nature’s beauty, Capitol Reef has something special for everyone. The scenic drives and historic orchards add to its unique charm. Plus, the park is home to diverse wildlife and amazing night skies perfect for stargazing.

Wow!

What is there to do?

Hike, camp, or just enjoy the scenic beauty as you drive through the park. There are dozens of trails ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes that lead to hidden canyons and arches. Camping under the stars here is a magical experience thanks to the dark skies. You can also explore the historic Fruita district with its charming orchards and visitor center. Photography enthusiasts will find endless inspiration in the vibrant colors and unique rock formations. Birdwatchers can spot a variety of desert and mountain birds. The park also hosts ranger-led programs during certain seasons to deepen your knowledge.

Fun Facts About the Park

  • It's an International Dark Sky Park – perfect for stargazing!
  • You can pick fresh fruit from historic orchards in Fruita.
  • The name comes from its white dome-shaped rock formations and the "reef" of cliffs that was once a barrier to travelers.

What Should You Know Before Coming?

Before visiting Capitol Reef, it’s important to know that the park is quite large and remote, so cell phone service can be limited. Make sure to bring plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months, as temperatures can soar above 100°F (38°C). The park is open year-round, but some roads and trails may be closed in winter due to snow. There are no gas stations inside the park, so fuel up beforehand. Visitors should also respect the fragile desert environment by staying on marked trails and not disturbing wildlife or historic sites. Camping is available, but reservations are recommended during busy seasons. Finally, check the National Park Service website for any alerts or special events before your trip.

Want to Learn More?

Capitol Reef National Park combines stunning landscapes with rich cultural history. Explore unique rock formations, ancient petroglyphs, scenic drives, and wildlife including mule deer, desert bighorn sheep, and golden eagles.
