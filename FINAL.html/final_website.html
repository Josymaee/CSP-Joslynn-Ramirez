The park is known for its colorful canyons, towering cliffs, domes, and natural bridges. One of its most distinctive features is the Waterpocket Fold, a nearly 100-mile-long wrinkle in the Earth’s crust that was formed millions of years ago. Early settlers named the area “Capitol Reef” because the white Navajo Sandstone domes looked like capitol building domes, and the rocky ridges reminded them of ocean reefs that were difficult to cross.Learn more about the Capital Dome.

Learn About the Park

Capitol Reef is a national park is located in south-central Utah, in the heart of red rock county.

3 Fun Facts About Capitol Reef National Park:

  • Capitol Reef is designated an International Dark Sky Park, offering some of the darkest night skies in the country, perfect for stargazing.
  • Capitol Reef National park has large fruit orchards, which visitors are allowed to pick fruit from when in season.
  • What makes the landscape of Capitol Reef so unique is the rocky unique formation that is “wrinkly” making it look like reefs that are found underwater. Hence the name.

Capitol Reef National Park is a remarkable place that combines stunning natural landscapes with rich cultural history. From its unique rock formations and the Waterpocket Fold to ancient petroglyphs and historic orchards, the park offers a diverse and unforgettable experience. Whether you're exploring its trails or learning about its past, Capitol Reef is a special destination worth discovering.
